# Project Liaison Guide

Guidelines for CNCF staff on how to effectively support and work with CNCF hosted projects.

## Understanding Your Role

As CNCF staff, you serve as a liaison between the foundation and its hosted projects. Your role is to:
- **Enable project success** through services and support
- **Remove obstacles** that prevent projects from thriving
- **Facilitate communication** between projects and the CNCF
- **Maintain vendor neutrality** in all interactions

## Working with Project Maintainers

### Initial Contact
- **Introduce yourself** and your role at CNCF
- **Understand project needs** before proposing solutions
- **Respect project autonomy** and decision-making processes
- **Establish regular communication** cadence

### Ongoing Support
- **Regular check-ins** to understand evolving needs
- **Proactive service offers** based on project stage and growth
- **Escalation support** for technical or community issues
- **Resource connections** to other projects and experts

## Services You Can Provide

### Technical Services
- **Infrastructure support** for CI/CD, hosting, and development tools
- **Security scanning** and vulnerability management
- **License scanning** and compliance assistance
- **Performance testing** and optimization resources

### Community & Growth
- **Marketing support** for releases and milestones
- **Event coordination** for project presence at conferences
- **Contributor recruitment** through CNCF channels
- **Mentorship program** connections

### Governance & Operations
- **Legal assistance** with licensing and IP issues
- **Trademark support** and brand management
- **Process facilitation** for governance decisions
- **Conflict resolution** when requested

## Communication Best Practices

### Effective Approaches
- **Listen first** - understand before proposing solutions
- **Be responsive** - acknowledge requests promptly even if resolution takes time
- **Stay informed** - keep up with project developments and community
- **Coordinate internally** - ensure CNCF team alignment on project support

### What to Avoid
- **Imposing solutions** - let projects drive their own decisions
- **Making commitments** beyond your authority
- **Taking sides** in project conflicts without formal request
- **Overwhelming maintainers** with too many offers or check-ins

## Escalation Guidelines

### When to Escalate
- **Legal or IP issues** that require CNCF legal counsel
- **Code of conduct violations** that affect multiple projects
- **Vendor neutrality concerns** that impact project health
- **Resource conflicts** between CNCF and project needs

### How to Escalate
1. **Document the situation** clearly and objectively
2. **Gather relevant context** from all involved parties
3. **Identify the appropriate escalation path** within CNCF
4. **Communicate timeline** to affected projects
5. **Follow up** to ensure resolution

## Success Metrics

### Project Health Indicators
- **Active contribution** and community growth
- **Healthy governance** with clear decision-making
- **Regular releases** and sustainable development
- **Positive community feedback** about CNCF support

### Your Effectiveness
- **Responsive communication** with projects
- **Successful service delivery** and problem resolution
- **Strong relationships** with project maintainers
- **Proactive identification** of project needs
